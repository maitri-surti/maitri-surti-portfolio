## Part 3
The third data visualization I chose here is a line graph displaying the government debt-to-GDP ratio trends for different continents from 1995 to 2019. By focusing on continental averages, I aimed to highlight how different regions experienced changes in government debt relative to GDP over time. Having all the individual countries in the line graph made it clustered and impossible for the users to gain insights from the graph. 

I chose line graph because I feel like they make it easier to identify specific time periods when drastic changes occur, as seen with the sharp drop in North America around 2019. Through this visualization, I hoped to show both convergence and divergence in debt ratios across regions. For instance, Asia and North America show higher debt-to-GDP levels compared to other continents, while Australia maintains a lower, more stable line throughout the years. Each continentâ€™s trend line shows unique characteristics. 

Initially I thought of using gradient colors for the graph to look better but then I realized that using colors with less contract can pose a challenge for the users to interpret the graph effectively. Henc, I used very varied and different colors for all the continents to avoid any sort of confusion. This choice of using line graph grouped by continents and varied colors ultimately provides a clearer picture of each continent's fiscal health trajectory.
